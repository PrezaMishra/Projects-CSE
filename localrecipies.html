Bisi Bele Bath

A flavorful one-pot meal from Bengaluru

Bisi Bele Bath

Ingredients:

1 cup rice ,½ cup toor dal,Mixed vegetables
Spices: coriander seeds, cumin seeds, dry red chilies, chana dal, ½ cup grated coconut Tamarind extract: 2 tbsp ,Jaggery: 1 tsp ,Mustard seeds, curry leaves, asafoetida

Preparation:
1.Cook rice, dal, and vegetables.
2.Make spice paste.Combine rice, dal, veggies, tamarind, jaggery, and paste.
3. Simmer 5-10 mins.Temper with mustard, curry leaves, and asafoetida

Ragi Mudde

A traditional, nutritious staple often served with sambar or spicy mutton curry

Ragi Mudde

Ingredients:

1 cup ragi flour, 2 cups water, Salt to taste

1. Boil water with a pinch of salt.
2. Gradually add ragi flour, stirring to avoid lumps
3. Cook on low heat, stirring to form a smooth dough.
4. Once cool, shape the dough into balls.
5. Serve hot with sambar, curry, or yogurt.

Pandi Curry (Coorg Pork Curry)

A spicy, flavorful pork curry from Coorg, infused with kachampuli (Coorg vinegar)

Pandi Curry (Coorg Pork Curry)

Ingredients:

Pork, onions, garlic, ginger, green chilies, curry leaves, kachampuli, and spices.

1. Marinate pork with spices and kachampuli.
2.Sauté onions, garlic, and spices.
3.Cook the marinated pork until tender.
4.Add kachampuli and simmer. Serve with rice or roti

Kadambattu

A soft, steamed rice ball, typically served with spicy curry or chutney

Kadambattu

Ingredients:

Rice flour, water, salt.

1. Boil water with salt. 2. Gradually add rice flour, stirring to form dough. 3. Shape the dough into small balls. 4. Steam the balls until cooked. Serve with curry or chutney.

Jolada Roti

A traditional roti made from jowar (sorghum), often served with curry or chutney.

Jolada Roti

Fried to crispy perfection, an unforgettable flavor!

Ingredients:

Anjan (Fish), Spices, Rice Flour

1.Mix jowar flour with water and salt to form a dough.
2.Roll the dough into flat rounds.
3.Cook on a hot griddle until golden brown. Serve with curry or chutney.

Enne Badnekai

A spicy, stuffed brinjal curry, popular in North Karnataka.

Enne Badnekai

Ingredients:

Brinjal (eggplant), onion, garlic, tamarind, spices (coriander, cumin, chili powder)

1.Stuff brinjals with a spicy onion mixture.
2.Cook stuffed brinjals in oil until tender.
3.Add tamarind and spices, cook until flavors meld. Serve with rice or roti.

Bangda Fry(Mackerel Fry)

A popular coastal dish of crispy fried mackerel, marinated with spices.

Bangda Fry(Mackerel Fry)

Ingredients:

Mackerel fish, turmeric, red chili powder, garlic, ginger, lime, salt.

1.Marinate the mackerel with spices and lime for 30 minutes.
2.Heat oil and fry the fish until crispy and golden brown. Serve with rice or curry.

Fish Gassi

A A rich and flavorful fish curry with a coconut base, commonly enjoyed in Udupi.

Fish Gassi

Ingredients:

Fish (such as kingfish or mackerel), coconut, tamarind, onions, tomatoes, garlic, ginger, spices

1.Grind coconut with spices to make a paste.
2.Cook onions, garlic, and tomatoes, then add the coconut paste and tamarind.
3.Add fish, cook until tender. Serve with rice or neer dosa.
